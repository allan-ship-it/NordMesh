How it works

From legacy VPN to zero-trust mesh
in four steps.

NordMesh replaces your VPN concentrator with a software-defined mesh. Every device gets a stable mesh IP. Every connection is encrypted end-to-end. Every access decision is policy-driven.

1
🚀
Deploy the control plane

NordMesh runs as EU-hosted SaaS or on your own infrastructure via Docker. EU DERP relay servers in Frankfurt, Amsterdam, and Copenhagen handle connection establishment — no traffic touches US infrastructure.

2
🔗
Connect your devices

Enrol laptops, servers, cloud instances, and mobile devices via a branded portal or QR code. WireGuard creates encrypted P2P tunnels automatically — including through NAT and firewalls. Up to 10 Gbps throughput on Linux.

3
🔐
Define access policies

Write fine-grained ACLs that specify exactly which users and devices can reach which resources. Authenticate via SSO/OIDC with Google, Microsoft Entra, or Okta. MagicDNS resolves all mesh devices automatically.

4
📊
Monitor and comply

Network flow logs, device posture checks, and NIS2-aligned audit trails are generated automatically. MSPs manage all client environments from a single console — with per-customer billing and reporting.

Why European CIOs should care

Regulatory momentum is
moving fast. Is your VPN ready?

Three EU frameworks have converted VPN modernisation from a best-practice to a legal obligation. NordMesh is built for this regulatory environment — not adapted to it.

NIS2
MFA on VPNs is now mandatory

NIS2 (transposing across EU member states throughout 2026) mandates MFA on all internet-facing systems, explicitly including VPNs. ENISA requires organisations to submit to audits by June 2026. NordMesh enforces MFA at the mesh layer and generates the evidence trail auditors need.

GDPR / Schrems II
VPN metadata is personal data

Network connection logs — who connected, from where, to what, when — are personal data under GDPR. If your VPN control plane is US-hosted, you may be in violation of Schrems II. NordMesh processes all coordination traffic within EU borders. Always.

Cyber Resilience Act
A third compliance wave arrives in 2026

The Cyber Resilience Act applies from September 2026, adding vulnerability management and incident reporting obligations for digital network products. NordMesh's audit logging and network topology reporting is designed to support CRA alignment from launch.

🇪🇺

EU sovereignty is no longer optional for enterprise procurement.

Public sector, healthcare, financial services, and critical infrastructure buyers across Europe are adding EU-hosted controls as mandatory procurement criteria — not optional preferences. NordMesh was designed for this reality. Every component of the stack — control plane, DERP relays, audit logs — operates within EU jurisdiction.
